    Between Two Lines | Motion Graphic Animation

    Between Two Lines is a final year project that tried to tackle and destigmatise the unintended pregnancy issue among teenagers and young adults in Taiwan by developing an informational platform that provides legitimate knowledge for people in need to understand the options and related consequences.

    To celebrate Mother’s Day in 2019 during the campaign, we came up with this new idea that allows people to regard unintended pregnancy from a different perspective and learn about the issue through a child’s point of view .

    In this project, I was in charge of Storyboard, Illustration/ animation components.

    2020 New Year Postcard

    Every year Taiwan Startup Stadium sends out New Year gifts along with the best wishes to their fellow startup members and business partners as a gesture to celebrate Lunar New Year. This year was no exception. With the inspiration from the Asian tradition, the initial pattern of the design symbolises the shape of Rat, which is the horoscope sign of the year. The colour scheme inherits the traditional cultural codes that represent fortune and prosperous with lower saturation and brightness. I tried to make the design simple, different from the typical New Year celebration vibe and more inclusive that not only can be used on Lunar New Year occasions but also the year of 2020 itself.

    Castnet Business Card

    Castnet, an independent online news media run by students of the Department of Communication and Technology at NCTU, is one of the mandatory professional training programmes designed by the Department for the juniors. The design of this business card follows the colour scheme of the official website of Castnet in order to cohere the brand image from the cyberspace to the real world. Instead of putting the logo of Castnet, which is merely a simple font-based logotype, directly into the design, I re-symbolised the idea of the media with a pen to enhance the association between Castnet and news media.

    The Cultural Pamphlet of Sii-Tam Town

    In recent years, with the support of Hakka Affair Council, Sii-Tam Town has been trying to increase its notability through a series of campaigns in hope of promoting their unique Hakka culture and attracting more people to visit.

    The Cultural Pamphlet of Sii-Tam is also part of the project. Divided into two issues, the pamphlets cover the historical sites of Sii-Tam and the individuals with mastery in traditional crafts. In collaboration with Kuanhsi 69 Bookstore, I was in charge of the editorial design for the two pamphlets.

    Startup Survival Kit

    Starting Lineup X Membership is a programme provided by Taiwan Startup Stadium for the early-stage startups in Taiwan that seek opportunities to expand their business overseas. To welcome the newly admitted, TSS gifts them several items in aggregate as a symbolic survival kit they need in this startup ecosystem. Therefore, we used the craft paper as the packaging material to represent the ruggedness of startup companies and portrayed the unknown adventure waiting ahead with jungle leaves.

    2018 National Chiao Tung University Undokai

    Originally from Japan, the spirit of Undokai, the sports day, isn't merely about individual honour and competition but also team-bonding and cooperation. In order to encourage as many people as possible, Undokai also offers a series of light-hearted team-building activities for everyone to be part of the event.

    Initially, the design of the poster utilised balls and running track as symbols to depict the imagery of track and field events respectively. After a few editions, I decided to combine the two elements altogether to represent one of the most common team-building games in Undokai - Odamakorogashi, Giant Balls Rolling, which requires a team to work together and roll the giant rubber ball towards the finish line - to portray this underline spirit of Undokai.

    Math and Art - A Popular Science Exhibition

    Mathematics has always been one of the most mysterious and fantastic discoveries of human beings over the centuries. However, when mentioning mathematics, most people only recall the tedious math exercises and stressful exams in high school, and it is often considered as the language that can only be spoken by the super genius alike people. However, mathematics isn't merely about calculating or logic, it is also capable of making plenty of splendid visual artworks with the use of geometrical methods. Professor Chen Ming Zhang, therefore, decided to hold this exhibition and introduced the concept of the “Structural Cloning” to the public by showing people the amazing artworks that have been done accordingly and explained the theory behind it.

    “Open Another Window Between Math and Art”

    This is the idea and the tagline raised by Professor Chen. In order to cohere the idea and present the concept of the structural cloning to the audience, the design of the poster brings out the similar style of artwork that relates to the structural cloning with the simple rectangle as the geometrical item that initiates the infinitive expansion and represents the idea of the window mentioned in the tagline.
